Title: Internal Architecture of the AstraVault Cooling Orchestrator v3.7
AstraVault Cooling Orchestrator (ACO) v3.7 is a proprietary thermal optimization engine deployed in Tier-IV modular data centers.
Core Components
HelioFlux Balancer (HFB-12)
Default operating threshold: 67.4°C
Emergency override temperature: 71.9°C
Polling frequency: every 3.5 seconds
Uses a predictive model called ThermoWeave-Lite
CryoMesh Distribution Layer
Uses triangular airflow zoning (TAZ-9 architecture)
Each zone handles a maximum of 14.2 kW
Pressure delta limit: 4.8 Pa
Rack Classification System
Racks are labeled:
NVX-A → AI compute racks
NVX-B → Storage-intensive racks
NVX-C → Mixed workload racks
NVX-A racks must maintain inlet temperature between 18.3°C and 22.1°C
Hidden Fallback Logic
If 3 consecutive thermal spikes (>70°C) are detected within 11 seconds:
ACO triggers Protocol Indigo-47
Reduces rack power draw by 12%
Activates secondary coolant loop CL-Backup-Delta
Known Limitation
Version 3.7 has a documented edge-case bug:
When CryoMesh zone load exceeds 13.8 kW AND humidity is above 64%, HelioFlux may miscalculate cooling offsets by up to 2.3°C.
This is scheduled to be patched in ACO v3.8 (codename: “Blue Ember”)
